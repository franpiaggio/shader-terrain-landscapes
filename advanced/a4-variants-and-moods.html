Variants & moods

One renderer, many worlds. The A3 landscape becomes a fiery sunset or a sun-baked desert by changing a handful of numbers — the sun, the palette, the materials, the height. Here are two worked examples and the knobs that make them.

Your win this lesson See how the same terrain code yields completely different places, and learn which few parameters carry the mood — so you can invent your own.

The knobs that change everything

Nothing structural changes between these — same march, same fBM, same normals. What changes:

  • Sun direction & colour — a low, deep-orange sun makes a sunset; a high white sun makes midday. Low sun = long shadows = drama.
  • Sky & fog palette — the sky gradient and the fog tint set the whole colour key.
  • Materials — swap grass/rock/snow for sand, or pink-lit snow.
  • Height & frequency — taller, lower-frequency fBM gives broad dunes; shorter, higher-frequency gives jagged ridges.

Sunset alpine

A low side sun (sun.y ≈ 0.16), an orange-to-violet sky, warm rim light and long cast shadows, snow tinted pink. Drag sun to slide it along the horizon and haze for the atmosphere.

Desert dunes

Same renderer, but the height is pow(fbm, 1.6) for rounded dunes, a single warm sand material with faint ripples, no snow, and a hot pale sky. dunes scales the height, sun moves the light.
