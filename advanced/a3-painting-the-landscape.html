Painting the landscape

Form plus light gives you a grey landform. To make it read as a place you paint it — grass on gentle ground, rock on the steeps, snow on the peaks — then add cast shadows, warm sun against cool shade, and atmospheric fog. That contrast is what sells it.

Your win this lesson Drive material color from slope & height, add a soft cast shadow and warm/cool lighting, and fly through a high-contrast landscape you can paste into Shadertoy.

Material from slope & height

Two numbers decide the material. The slope is normal.y (1 = flat, 0 = vertical), the height is the normalized terrain value:

vec3 mat = mix(rock, grass, smoothstep(0.55, 0.82, slope));       // steep → rock, flat → grass
mat = mix(mat, snow, smoothstep(snowLine, snowLine+0.2, h01)      // high...
                     * smoothstep(0.5, 0.78, slope));              // ...and not a sheer cliff

Contrast: shadow, warm vs cool, deep sky

A flat-lit landscape looks washed out. Three cheap moves fix that:

  • Cast shadows — march a ray from each point toward the sun; if terrain blocks it, darken. Big, dramatic shadows across the valleys.
  • Warm sun, cool shade — light the lit side with a warm tint and fill the shadow with cool sky-blue, not grey. The colour contrast reads as sunlight.
  • A deeper sky + tighter fog — saturated blue overhead, and fog that only takes the far distance, instead of a pale wash over everything.
